Hockey Overview

Hockey is a fast-paced team sport played on ice, field, or in a rink, where players use sticks to hit a puck or ball into the opposing team's goal. It is popular worldwide and comes in several variations, including ice hockey and field hockey.

Key Elements of Hockey
  • Players:

    Two teams, typically with 6 players each in ice hockey or 11 in field hockey.

  • Objective:

    Score goals by hitting the puck or ball into the opponent's net.

  • Duration:

    Ice hockey games are divided into three 20-minute periods; field hockey games consist of two 35-minute halves.

  • Equipment:

    Sticks, skates (for ice hockey), helmets, and protective gear.

Major Hockey Leagues and Events
  • Ice Hockey:

    National Hockey League (NHL), Winter Olympics, IIHF World Championship

  • Field Hockey:

    Hockey World Cup, FIH Pro League, Summer Olympics

Objective

The goal is to score by getting the puck or ball into the opponent’s net.

Team Structure
  • Ice Hockey:

    6 players per team.

  • Field Hockey:

    11 players per team.

Game Duration
  • Ice Hockey:

    Three 20-minute periods.

  • Field Hockey:

    Two 35-minute halves.

Early History

Hockey’s origins can be traced back to ancient civilizations such as Egypt, Greece, and Persia, where stick-and-ball games were played as early as 4,000 years ago. These early forms laid the foundation for what would eventually evolve into modern hockey.

Field hockey, as we know it today, emerged in England during the 18th century. It gained popularity in schools and clubs, leading to the establishment of the Hockey Association in London in 1886, which standardized the rules of the game.

Hockey Rules and Regulations

Objective of the Game

The goal of hockey is to score more goals than the opposing team by shooting the puck or ball into the opponent's net using a hockey stick.



Team Composition
  • Ice Hockey:

    6 players per team on the ice (including a goalie).

  • Field Hockey:

    11 players per team on the field.

Game Duration
  • Ice Hockey:

    Three 20-minute periods, with breaks in between.

  • Field Hockey:

    Two 35-minute halves or four 15-minute quarters (depending on the tournament).

4. Scoring

A goal is scored when the puck or ball crosses the goal line inside the net. Goals must be scored legally, adhering to the rules of the game.

Basic Rules

  • Players must not touch the puck or ball with their hands, except for goalkeepers.

  • No high-sticking (raising the stick above shoulder height).

  • Body checking is allowed in ice hockey but prohibited in field hockey.

  • Fouls such as tripping, hooking, or slashing result in penalties or free hits.

Penalties

Penalties are given for infractions like rough play, dangerous moves, or unsportsmanlike conduct. Players may serve time in the penalty box (ice hockey) or concede penalty corners (field hockey).

Overtime and Shootouts

If the game is tied, overtime periods or shootouts may determine the winner, depending on the competition format.

Equipment

The hockey stick is used by players to hit the puck or ball. In ice hockey, the stick has a flat blade for controlling the puck, while in field hockey, the blade is curved for better ball control. The stick’s length and curve are tailored to the player's style and position.
